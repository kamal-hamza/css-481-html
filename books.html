Books That Defined My Childhood

Why These Books Matter to Me

Reading has been a fundamental part of my childhood and it continues to be important today. These three book series offer adventure, humor, and magic that have shaped my love for reading.

Below are the series I've read multiple times and continue to recommend to everyone.

Series 1: Harry Potter

The Magical Series That Started It All

I've read the entire Harry Potter series three times, and each reading reveals new details and deeper meaning. This series basically introduced me to the world of fantasy literature and got me hooked on reading.

"It does not do to dwell on dreams and forget to live."

— Albus Dumbledore, Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone
Series Information
Author
J.K. Rowling
Number of Books
7 main novels
First Published
June 26, 1997
Genre
Fantasy, Adventure
Setting
Wizarding World, primarily Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry
All Seven Books in Order
  1. Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone (Philosopher's Stone in UK)
  2. Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets
  3. Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban
  4. Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire *My favorite*
  5. Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix
  6. Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince
  7. Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows
Why I Love This Series
  • Rich world-building with incredible detail
  • Complex characters that grow throughout the series
  • Themes of friendship, courage, and standing up for what's right
  • Perfect balance of humor, adventure, and emotional depth
  • Each book gets progressively more mature with the readers (which is pretty cool)
Key Themes
Love and Sacrifice
The power of love, especially a mother's love, is the strongest magic.
Good vs Evil
The eternal struggle and the choices that define us.
Friendship
Harry, Ron, and Hermione's friendship is the heart of the series.
Prejudice and Discrimination
Examining blood purity beliefs and house-elf rights.

Series Rating: 5 out of 5 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Series 2: Geronimo Stilton

Adventures of a Mouse Journalist

The Geronimo Stilton series is a very nostalgic collection of adventures that combines humor, education, and colorful storytelling. It was also the first ever book series I read back in elementary school so it holds a special place in my heart.

Series Information
Author
Elisabetta Dami (pen name: Geronimo Stilton)
Main Character
Geronimo Stilton, editor of The Rodent's Gazette
First Published
2000
Number of Books
80+ books in the main series
Setting
New Mouse City, Mouse Island
What Makes This Series Special
  • Unique typography with different fonts and colors (made reading super fun)
  • Colorful illustrations on every page
  • Short chapters that are easy to digest (perfect for younger readers)
Main Characters
Geronimo Stilton
The nervous but kind-hearted protagonist and narrator
Thea Stilton
Geronimo's adventurous sister and special correspondent
Trap Stilton
Geronimo's prankster cousin who loves food
Benjamin
Geronimo's favorite nephew, sweet and curious
Favorite Books from the Series
  1. Lost Treasure of the Emerald Eye (Book 1)
  2. Attack of the Bandit Cats
  3. Journey Through Time *Special Edition*

Series Rating: 4.5 out of 5 ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Fun Fact

The books use over 100 different fonts to make reading more engaging! Words are styled to match their meaning.

The Hilarious Middle School Chronicles

Diary of a Wimpy Kid by Jeff Kinney is a really funny series that was just super entertaining to read because of all the hilarious stories the main character (Greg) would tell.

Reading Format

The books are written as journals (not diaries!) with Greg's commentary and stick-figure drawings. This format makes them difficult easy and fun to read!

"I'll be famous one day, but for now I'm stuck in middle school with a bunch of morons."

Impact on My Life

These book series have taught me valuable lessons:

From Harry Potter

  • The importance of friendship and loyalty
  • Courage in the face of adversity
  • Love is the most powerful magic

From Geronimo Stilton

  • Adventure can be found anywhere
  • Learning can be fun and exciting
  • Family and friends make everything better

From Diary of a Wimpy Kid

  • It's okay to be imperfect
  • Middle school struggles are universal
  • Humor helps us cope with challenges
